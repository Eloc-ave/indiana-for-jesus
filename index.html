What We Believe

Our Statement of Faith

Indiana For Jesus stands on the historic, biblical Christian faith. Here is a summary of what we believe.

God

  • There is one eternal, self-existent God who created and sustains all things. Deuteronomy 6:4; Isaiah 40:28
  • God exists as three Persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — each fully God, equal in glory and majesty. Matthew 3:15–17; John 1:1–3
  • God created the heavens, the earth, and all things by his own will and for his own glory. Genesis 1:1–2:3; Colossians 1:16

Jesus Christ

  • Jesus is the eternal Son of God who became fully human while remaining fully God. John 1:1–3, 1:14; Philippians 2:5–8
  • He was conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the virgin Mary, and lived a sinless life. Isaiah 7:14; Matthew 1:16–23; 2 Corinthians 5:21
  • He died on the cross as a complete and sufficient payment for the sin of mankind. John 19:30; Hebrews 10:12–14; 1 John 2:2
  • He rose bodily from the dead, ascended to heaven, and now intercedes for believers at the right hand of the Father. 1 Corinthians 15:3–5; Hebrews 7:25
  • He will return in person to judge the living and the dead. Acts 17:31; 2 Timothy 4:1

The Holy Spirit

  • The Holy Spirit is the third Person of the Godhead, who gives life and glorifies Christ. John 15:26, 16:14
  • He convicts the world of sin and draws sinners to repent and believe the Gospel. John 16:8–11; Titus 3:3–7
  • He lives within every believer, producing the fruit of righteousness in their life. Romans 8:11; Galatians 5:22–23

Scripture

  • The 66 books of the Bible are God's inspired, inerrant, and fully authoritative Word. 2 Timothy 3:16–17
  • Scripture is sufficient and trustworthy in everything it addresses, including faith and history. 2 Timothy 3:16–17
  • Everything necessary for salvation is clearly taught in Scripture. Romans 6:23, 10:9; Acts 16:30–31

Creation

  • Genesis presents a true historical account of how God created the world and all life. Genesis 1–11
  • God created the world in six ordinary days, and humanity has existed for around six thousand years. Exodus 20:8–11; Hebrews 4:3–4
  • Mankind did not evolve from other life but was specially and directly created by God. Genesis 2:7, 2:21–23; 1 Corinthians 15:39

Mankind

  • Every person — male and female — is made in the image of God and carries equal worth and dignity. Genesis 1:26–27; James 3:9–10
  • Human life is sacred from the moment of conception and must be protected and honored. Psalm 51:5; Jeremiah 1:5
  • All people descend from Adam and Eve and belong to one human race. Genesis 3:20; Acts 17:26
  • God designed marriage as the lifelong union of one man and one woman, and intends sexual intimacy for that covenant alone. Genesis 2:24; Matthew 19:4–5
  • A person's sex is determined by God at conception and cannot be changed; this truth is shared with compassion, pointing all people toward the restoration found in Christ. Psalm 139:13–16; Acts 3:19–21

Sin

  • Because of Adam's fall, all people are born sinful and personally choose to sin. Romans 5:12; Psalm 116:11
  • Sin separated humanity from God and brought death, suffering, and brokenness into the world. Genesis 3:17–19; Romans 8:20–22
  • Every person stands equally in need of the salvation that is found only in Jesus Christ. Romans 3:23; 2 Peter 3:9

Salvation

  • Salvation is a free gift of God's grace, received through faith in Jesus Christ alone. Ephesians 2:8–9
  • It cannot be earned by good works, but comes through repentance and trust in Christ's finished work. Titus 3:3–7; 1 Peter 1:3
  • Anyone who turns from sin and receives Jesus as Savior and Lord is born again as a child of God. John 1:12–13; Acts 3:19

The Church

  • The Church is made up of all who trust in Christ, united together as his body. Romans 10:8–10; Ephesians 5:23
  • Those who reject Christ face eternal separation from God, while believers are secure in eternal life with him. John 3:16–18, 3:36
  • Christians are called to gather with a local, Bible-believing church to worship, grow, and love one another. Hebrews 10:23–25, 13:17

Christ's Return

  • Jesus will return in person to fulfill his promises and establish his kingdom. Acts 1:9–11; 1 Peter 4:5
  • All people will be raised — believers to eternal life, and those who reject Christ to eternal judgment. Daniel 12:2; 1 Corinthians 15:12–26
  • Satan and all evil will ultimately be judged and defeated forever. Matthew 25:41; Revelation 20:10

Hell

  • Hell is a real place of eternal judgment for all who do not repent and believe in Jesus Christ. John 3:18, 3:36; Revelation 21:8
  • It is a place of weeping and gnashing of teeth, conscious and unending separation from God. Matthew 13:49–50, 25:41–46
